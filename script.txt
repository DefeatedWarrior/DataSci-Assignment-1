Slide 1 – Title Slide
Introduce yourself and the project
Explain that the story explores teen social media use, wellbeing, and the under-16 law

Slide 2 – Introduction
Social media plays a big role in teen life
Teens use it for entertainment, connection, and belonging
Australia has new restrictions for under-16s to protect health and wellbeing
Purpose: see if social media trends support these restrictions

Slide 3 – User Persona
Liam, 15, Canberra
Uses social media a lot, especially late at night
Follows trends and enjoys memes
Needs: understand the law, see social media impact, learn tips to balance screen time

Slide 4 – Problem Statement
Liam wants to manage his social media use
Facing potential negative effects of screen time
New law limits access for under-16s

Slide 5 – Dear Data
Visual summary of app usage over ten days
Outer ring shows time spent per app
Inner rings show number of opens and notifications
Highlights patterns of attention and engagement

Slide 6 – Behaviour: Notifications and Usage
More notifications lead to more app opens and longer usage
App usage is influenced by alerts, not just personal choice
Teens often check apps without realizing it due to notifications

Slide 7 – Attention: Dominant Apps
Small number of apps take up most screen time
Mostly social media and entertainment apps
Platforms are designed to capture attention and keep users engaged

Slide 8 – Comparison: Liam and Other Students
Liam’s usage is similar to peers
High screen time is common among teens
Peer pressure and desire for social acceptance drive engagement
Shows the trend is widespread, not just one individual

Slide 9 – Impact: Mental Health and Sleep
Higher usage is linked to lower mental health scores
Higher usage is linked to fewer sleep hours
Indicates potential negative effects, even if not direct causation

Slide 10 – Apps versus Screen Time
More apps installed often means more total screen time
Social media and entertainment apps dominate usage
Utility and productivity apps contribute little
Type of app strongly influences total usage

Slide 11 – Trends, Variability, Bias and Ethics
Notifications drive engagement
Usage varies by app, day, and individual habits
Limitations: single user, short timeframe
Ethical considerations: app design and government restrictions

Slide 12 – Conclusion
Social media use is strongly influenced by app design and notifications
Excessive usage may affect health and sleep
Restrictions for younger users are supported by the data
Helps teens make informed choices and balance connection with control
